Conjunction

The rules for conjunction are the following: $$\infer=[\land Intro]{A\land B}{A&B}$$ $$\infer=[\land Elim] {A} {A\land B}$$ $$\infer=[\land Elim] {B} {A\land B}$$.

In LEAN if the rules function very similarly. To extract from your hypothesis $$h: A \land B$$ the $$A$$ then you enter $$And.right $$ $$ h$$. Similarly for $$B$$ you write $$And.left $$ $$ h$$. To introduce the $$\land$$ you write $$And.intro$$ $$ A $$ $$ B$$ to get $$A \land B $$ or $$And.intro $$ $$ hA $$ $$ hB$$ if $$hA, hB$$ are your hypotheses respectively.
