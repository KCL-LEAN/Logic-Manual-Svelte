Conjunction

The rules for conjunction are the following: $$\infer=[\land Intro]{A\land B}{A&B}$$ $$\infer=[\land Elim] {A} {A\land B}$$ $$\infer=[\land Elim] {B} {A\land B}$$.

In LEAN if the rules function very similarly. To extract from your hypothesis $$h: A \land B$$ the $$A$$ then you enter 

and.right h
Similarly for B you write 
and.left h
To introduce the $$\land$$ you write 
and.intro A B
to get $$A \land B $$ or 
and.intro hA hB
if $$hA, hB$$ are your hypotheses respectively. This is an example natural deduction proof involving the rules for conjunction:

Example: $$B \land A \vdash A \land B$$ $$\infer=[\land Intro] {A\land B} {\infer=[\land Elim] {A} {B \land A} & \infer=[\land Elim]{B} {B \land A}} $$ Now compare this to the derivation in Lean.
